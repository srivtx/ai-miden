# How to Navigate the Nature Curriculum

> Read this first. It tells you *what to read, in what order, and why*.

---

## The 30-second version

This is a **bottom-up, then top-down** curriculum. Read in this order:

1. **Part 0** (First Principles) — read 4 files
2. **Part 1** (Building Blocks) — read 6 files
3. **Part 2** (Learning Mechanisms) — read 6 files
4. **Part 3** (Architecture) — read 7 files
5. **Part 4** (Systems) — read 5 files
6. **Part 5** (Comparison) — read 1 file (synthesis)
7. **Part 6** (Signals from Scratch) — read 5 files (deepens 1-2)
8. **Part 7** (When the Brain Breaks) — read 5 files
9. **Part 8** (Measuring the Brain) — read 5 files
10. **Part 9** (Brain to Mind) — read 6 files
11. **Part 10** (Bio-Inspired AI) — read 5 files
12. **Part 11** (Implementations) — read code, run it

**Total: ~70 files, ~50,000 words, ~25 hours of reading.**

---

## The 4 reading paths

Different readers want different things. Pick your path.

### Path A: "I want to understand my brain" (1 weekend)
- Part 0 (4 files): signals, information, gradients
- Part 1 (6 files): the physical substrate
- Part 5 (1 file): the comparison
- Part 9 (1-2 files): consciousness
- **Total: ~12 files, ~6 hours**

### Path B: "I'm an AI researcher, give me the bio" (3 days)
- Part 5 first (synthesis)
- Part 1, 2 (substrate + learning)
- Part 3, 4 (architecture + systems)
- Part 6 (signals — this is what AI is missing)
- Part 9 (consciousness)
- Part 10 (bio-inspired AI)
- **Total: ~30 files, ~20 hours**

### Path C: "I want to build brain-like AI" (1 week)
- Part 0 (principles)
- Part 2 (learning rules)
- Part 6 (signals — multi-factor, neuromodulation)
- Part 10 (bio-inspired AI)
- Part 11 (implementations)
- **Total: ~20 files + code, ~15 hours**

### Path D: "I'm a student, give me the full curriculum" (2-3 weeks)
- Read in order from Part 0 through Part 11
- Do the exercises
- Run the implementations in Part 11
- **Total: ~70 files, ~25 hours of reading + exercises**

## The pedagogical principles

Every file follows this template (so you know what to expect):

1. **What is X?** — title
2. **The Problem** — why does this concept exist? What pain does it solve?
3. **Definition** — in one or two simple sentences
4. **How It Works (Step-by-Step)** — 4-6 numbered steps
5. **Real-life analogy** — concrete everyday example
6. **Tiny numeric example** — specific numbers, actual math
7. **Common confusion** — 6+ "No." bullets
8. **Key properties** — 4-5 bullets
9. **Where it appears in technology** — comparison to AI
10. **Connection to next file** — what to read next, and why
