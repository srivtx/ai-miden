# Part 9: Brain to Mind

> The deepest question. The brain is wet chemistry. The mind is — what? Conscious? Free? Self? This part explores the *hardest* questions: what is consciousness? What is the self? What is free will? What are qualia? These are the questions at the frontier.

## The arc

These six questions are all facets of the same deep problem: **how does a physical system (the brain) produce a subjective experience (the mind)?** Each file approaches it from a different angle:
- **Consciousness** is the umbrella
- **Global workspace** is the cognitive neuroscience theory
- **Integrated information** is the mathematical theory
- **Free will** is the agency question
- **Self** is the identity question
- **Qualia** is the experiential question

None of these has a settled answer. But the *convergence* of neuroscience, philosophy, and AI is producing real progress. The Global Workspace Theory has empirical support (Dehaene, Baars). Integrated Information has a formal framework (Φ is computable in principle). Predictive coding (Part 4) provides a unifying computational framework. The "hard problem" remains — but we know more than we did 20 years ago.

## Why this matters for AI

If you build an AI that is *conscious* (whatever that means), the question is not technical. It is *philosophical and ethical*. The IIT and GWS give us *operational* tests: does this system have high Φ? Does it have a global workspace broadcasting information? If yes, the AI may be conscious. If no, it is probably not.

This is no longer academic. As AI systems become more sophisticated, these questions become urgent. Part 10 (Bio-Inspired AI) will return to this question.
