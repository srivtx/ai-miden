# Part 7: When the Brain Breaks

> The most powerful way to understand a system is to see what happens when it fails. Brain disorders — Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, schizophrenia, autism, depression — are not just diseases. They are *natural experiments* that reveal which brain systems are necessary for which functions. Each disorder is a window into the brain's architecture.

## The arc

Each disorder reveals a specific subsystem:
- **Alzheimer's:** the hippocampus (memory) and cortex (cognition)
- **Parkinson's:** the basal ganglia (movement) and substantia nigra (dopamine)
- **Schizophrenia:** the dopamine system (positive symptoms) and prefrontal cortex (negative symptoms)
- **Depression:** the monoamine systems (serotonin, NE, DA), the HPA axis (stress), and BDNF (growth)
- **Autism:** the social brain, theory of mind, and synaptic pruning

This is the *systems view* of brain disorders: each disease is a *localized failure* of a specific subsystem. Modern neuroscience increasingly challenges this view — many disorders involve *multiple* systems, and *network-level* failures — but the localized-failure model remains a useful starting point.

## Why this matters

For AI, understanding brain disorders is important because:
1. **Disease models are computational models.** Each disorder is a *natural experiment* in which one part of the system is broken. The remaining function tells you what that part does.
2. **Many brain disorders are brain-network disorders.** They resemble *connectivity failures* in a neural network — a node is damaged, a connection is lost. The same architecture (modular + recurrent) is used in ANNs.
3. **Treatments are signals.** Drugs that *fix* a disorder (e.g., L-DOPA for Parkinson's, SSRIs for depression) are essentially *modulating* specific neurotransmitters — the same chemicals that Parts 1-6 covered. They are real-world experiments in the molecular cascade.
4. **Mental health and AI are converging.** AI is being used to predict, diagnose, and treat brain disorders. The same architectures that power AI are being used to *model* disorders.
