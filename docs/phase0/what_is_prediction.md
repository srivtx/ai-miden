# What Is Prediction?

## 1. Definition (very simple)

**Prediction is making a good guess about something you don't know yet, based on things you do know.**

That is the whole idea.

If your friend always arrives 10 minutes late, you can *predict* they will be late again today. You don't know it for sure, but you are using what you already know (their past behavior) to guess the future.

---

## 2. Why it exists

We need to make decisions about the future, or about values we cannot measure right now.

We cannot know everything in advance. We don't know tomorrow's weather, next month's prices, or whether a customer will like a new product. But we still need to plan. So we use the information we *do* have to make the best possible guess.

Prediction helps us act wisely even when we are uncertain.

---

## 3. Real-life analogy

Think of a **weather forecast**.

A meteorologist (a weather person) looks at things they can measure *right now*:

- Cloud cover
- Wind speed
- Temperature
- Air pressure

Using these known facts, they **predict** whether it will rain tomorrow.

They do not *know* the future. They are looking at patterns. When pressure drops and dark clouds gather, rain usually follows. So they say: "There is a 70% chance of rain tomorrow."

That is a prediction. A smart guess based on current knowledge.

---

## 4. Tiny numeric example

Here is a concrete, everyday example.

You have eaten at your favorite restaurant three times. Each time you ordered the pasta dish, the bill was:

| Visit | Pasta Price |
|-------|-------------|
| 1     | $12         |
| 2     | $13         |
| 3     | $11         |

You are going again tonight. You don't know the exact price this time, so you **predict** it will cost around **$12**.

How did you get that? You looked at the past examples: $12, $13, $11. They are all close to $12. So $12 is a reasonable guess.

You do not *know* it will be $12. Maybe the price went up. Maybe there is a special discount. But based on what you know, **$12 is your prediction**.

---

## 5. Common confusion

**A prediction is NOT a fact.**

This is important. A prediction is an educated guess. It can be wrong.

If the pasta actually costs $15 tonight, your prediction of $12 was incorrect. That does not mean predicting was useless. It means the guess was off, and that is normal.

The goal is not to be perfect every single time. The goal is to make your guesses **as close to correct as possible, as often as possible**.

Even weather forecasts are sometimes wrong. That is okay. We still use them because they are right *most* of the time, and that helps us plan.

---

## 6. Where it is used in our code

In our AI system, prediction plays the same role.

First, the computer looks at many past examples (this is called **learning**). It searches for patterns in the data, just like you noticed the pasta prices were always near $12.

Then, when we give it something new it has never seen before, it uses those patterns to make a **prediction**.

**The final output of our code is a prediction**: a guess about something unknown. It might be a number, a category, or a yes/no answer. Whatever it is, remember: it is a guess based on past examples, not a guaranteed fact.
