§ 01 The five principles
P-01
Multi-system memory
A fast hippocampal-like store captures every input one-shot. A slow cortical-like store holds structured, integrated knowledge. Two stores, one engine.
BrainFast + Slow
P-02
Replay consolidation
A nightly sleep() replays recent captures, deduplicates, clusters, and binds them to existing knowledge. The system thinks while you sleep.
SleepOffline
P-03
Neuromodulator gating
DA rewards retrieval of useful items. ACh gates the encoding of novel ones. NE controls gain. The chemistry of attention, in code.
DA · ACh · NEGated
P-04
Homeostatic pruning
Items unused for 90 days are pruned. The system forgets, like cortex prunes. Bounded storage, fresh signal.
Prune90d
P-05
Intrinsic motivation
surprise() surfaces the item least similar to recent context. Drives exploration of forgotten material. Curiosity, implemented.
CuriosityNovelty
P-06
Variational inference
The math: variational free energy minimized at every time scale. Same algorithm as the brain's, same algorithm as diffusion. One math, many systems.

§ 03 The science
MemPal is built on the Multi-Scale Predictive Coding Hypothesis (MSPCH), an original thesis developed in ai-miden/docs/nature/12_unification/MSPCH.md. The claim: the brain runs the same variational inference algorithm at every time scale from milliseconds to decades, with neuromodulators setting parameters and molecular cascades implementing each scale. Parts 0-12 of the nature/ curriculum in this repo (95,000+ words) provide the full evidence base. One math, many systems. That's the bet.
For researchers: the nature/ and gm/ curricula in this repo are the research backing. A user study (n=20, 2-week within-subject) is the next step. For everyone else: the system is daily-useful today. Capture. Sleep. Search. Surprise. Source on GitHub.
